    Accountable/Ownership
  • Acknowledges the impact of his work and mistakes on customers and the company.
  • Owns his tasks and projects from start to end (even when the responsibility is distributed between different people).
  • Asks for help when needed.
  • Admits and reports mistakes.
    • QUESTION 1:
    Our fraud analysts are responsible for POC (proof of concept) with new customers. Basically, this means – showing the customer that our models work, and we are able to prevent fraud. In order to do that we train our models with the customer's data. Imagine a case where you are the analyst in charge of the POC. The quality of data that you get from the customer isn’t good enough, thus the model results are not accurate enough. You are working closely with the customer's sales manager, which is pressuring you to authorize the model so we can officially start working with the customer. He tells you he made it clear to the customer that model accuracy will improve with time and data quality and that this customer is a strategic one and we must close the deal with him before our competitors. What would you do?
    Score the answer: *Give High Score
    *Give High Score *Understands the complexity of the situation, that there are pros and cons to each alternative. Does not see this as an easy decision.
    *Acknowledges their responsibility for model accuracy and the repercussions on the customer and Forter in case of authorizing an inaccurate model.
    *Does not authorize the model before getting to a reasonable accuracy level.
    *Insists on clearly managing expectations regarding model accuracy directly with the customer.
    QUESTION 2:
    One of your customers calls you and complains that model accuracy is not high enough – she says that your competitors are promising 95% accuracy and that your model barely approaches 80%. The customer asks you to commit to increase model accuracy to 95% within the upcoming quarter, otherwise, she will leave for the competitor. You know that reaching this level of accuracy is not possible, but you don't want to lose the customer. What would you do?
    Score the answer: *Give High Score
    *Give High Score *Will not commit to something they can't deliver.
    *Will take the time to explain the complexity to the customer.
    *Will commit to do the best of their ability to improve model accuracy and keep the customer in the loop.
    *Will escalate to a manager if needed.
